What is A11y?

Accessibility (A11y) is not just a technical requirement: it's an ethical commitment and a strategic advantage.

It aims to make digital products accessible to everyone, regardless of their abilities.

Why "a11y"?

A numeronym for the word accessibility:
"a" + 11 letters + "y"

Similar examples:
i18n (internationalization),
l10n (localization)

Accessibility is not an optional feature, it's a fundamental digital right for all users.

~15-20% of the global population has permanent or temporary disability: 1 person out to 5, which means 1.64B people

This involves

  • Visual impairments
  • Motor disabilities
  • Hearing loss
  • Cognitive limitations
  • Temporary or situational limitations
  • Environmental access challenges

Vision and Core Goals

  • Social Inclusion: Designing for everyone, without exception.
  • Enhanced UX: Clear, friendly, intuitive interfaces.
  • Innovation: Creative solutions to remove digital barriers.

Legal Framework

  • WCAG: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
  • ADA: Americans with Disabilities Act
  • EN 301 549: European accessibility standard
  • European Accessibility Act
  • AGID Declaration of Accessibility

Beyond Permanent Disabilities

Accessibility also supports:

  • Temporary conditions (e.g., broken limb)
  • Environmental limitations (e.g., poor lighting)
  • Technological barriers (e.g., old devices, slow internet)

Shared Responsibility

All stakeholders contribute to accessibility:

  • Designers
  • Developers
  • Project Managers
  • Testers & QA
  • C-level Executives

The Ethical Imperative

  • Digital Equity: Access to digital resources is a right.
  • Inclusion: No one left behind.
  • Social Responsibility: Design that serves society.
  • Better UX: Everyone benefits.
  • SEO: Accessibility improves visibility.

The Legal Imperative

Non-compliance leads to:

  • Fines
  • Legal action
  • Reputation loss

EU’s EAA promotes anonymous reporting of accessibility violations.

The Functional Imperative

Accessible design leads to:

  • Better usability
  • Improved performance
  • SEO and compatibility gains
  • Reduced technical debt

Core Principles of A11y - POUR

  • ✅ Perceivable: Content available to all senses
  • ✅ Operable: Interfaces usable without mouse
  • ✅ Understandable: Language and interactions are intuitive
  • ✅ Robust: Works with various technologies

Accessibility is not just for the web — it's a universal design principle.

Final Thoughts

Building accessible UI and UX for digital products means building for everyone! Let’s shape an inclusive digital world.
